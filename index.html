Key Finding
The discursive space where AI is imagined as enabling genuinely transformative sustainability futures, the "AI for the Future" zone, exists theoretically but is largely unoccupied. The technology marketed as sustainability's greatest enabler may be foreclosing the very imagination sustainability demands.

Preliminary Methodology

Critical Discourse Analysis (Fairclough, 1992) applied to naturalistic AI-sustainability discourse

Purpose

How do imagined AI futures activate or constrain futures-oriented thinking?

This study investigates how AI imaginaries circulating in sustainability discourse activate or suppress futures consciousness dimensions, and how these patterns construct particular leadership orientations. It examines naturalistic discourse on YouTube and Reddit to capture what practitioners actually say when not responding to a researcher's prompt.

Gümüsay & Reinecke, 2022; Mische, 2009
Research Questions

RQ1

How are the dimensions of futures consciousness discursively represented or suppressed in online AI-sustainability discourse?

RQ2

How does the discursive framing of AI for sustainability construct particular leadership orientations along the operational-enabling spectrum?

Futures Consciousness

Individual Analytical Lens (Textual Level)

Five dimensions describing a person's capacity to engage meaningfully with the future: Time Perspective, Agency Beliefs, Openness to Alternatives, Systems Perception, and Concern for Others.

Ahvenharju et al., 2018; Lalot et al., 2021
AI Imaginaries

Collective Visions (Discursive Practice Level)

Sociotechnical imaginaries classified along two dimensions: valence (utopian/dystopian) and orientation toward change (instrumental/transformative). Yields the 2x2 typology shown in the matrix above.

Jasanoff & Kim, 2015; Hultin & Mahring, 2025
Complexity Leadership Theory

Leadership Orientations (Social Practice Level)

Operational leadership (managing, optimising, deploying) vs. Enabling leadership (creating adaptive spaces for emergence and transformation).

Uhl-Bien & Arena, 2018

What This Means

Ethical concern without agency is the discursive recipe for operational leadership

Discourse can be ethically concerned (high Concern for Others, 4:1 activated) while simultaneously being futures-unconscious (suppressed Agency Beliefs, near-balanced Time Perspective). Speakers routinely express care but position AI futures as inevitable rather than chosen. This produces the discursive conditions under which operational leadership ("we care, but we can't change the trajectory") appears natural, and enabling leadership ("we can shape AI's role in sustainability") appears impractical.
